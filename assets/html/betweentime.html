Between Time

Between Time is a coop-puzzle-game built after the concept of escape rooms. The players work together while being in different dimensions: Player one is in a dimension between the times via virtual reality while player two is in the real world and has a controller to interact with the other dimension. The players must communicate with each other to complete the puzzles within time and save the dimensions from crumbling.

Images of the Models

Background of Between Time

Between Time was developed in a small group of 3 over the course of a semester. I worked with Steven Sachtleben and Malte Kasolowsky together and while they focused on the programming of the game, I was responsible for the 3D models.

Modelling Process

In the beginning we decided on a simplistic and low-poly style for the models with a simple plain colour texture to prevent lag in the game. Therefore all the models look quite simple in their form and detail. Nevertheless I tried to give each model some kind of detail to focus on. As it is the first puzzle, I started with the models for the Mayan puzzle. The pyramid is built out of simple cuboids with more detailed stairs as a focus point as they are also integral for the result of the puzzle and for the solving process of the next one. Form the medieval themed models the altar with the candles and the book on top is the focus point as it is also the main part of the puzzle. The steampunk puzzle looks impressive when you stand in front of it because of the huge maze out of pipes. Because the pipes are already so detailed I also put a lot of metal rings around the machine the maze comes from, so that it doesn’t fade into the background next to the labyrinth. On the middle-platform are 3 pedestals for the timecubes, each modelled in style of their representing timeline to distinguish clearly where which timecube earned from which puzzle belongs. In the end I worked on some backgorund models that float slowly around the islands during the game to distract the players from the important items in the foreground and to give the background some interest.
