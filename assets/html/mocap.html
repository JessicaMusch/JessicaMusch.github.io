Between Dice and Madness: A classic Adventure

Between Dice and Madness: A classic Adventure was mainly developed during my 6th semester. The goal of our assignment was a short movie that included animations recorded with motion capture and is at least 3 minutes long. During our script writing, we decided on shooting a 10-minute-long movie, as we could not portray the complete story within only 3 minutes.

As some actors were not comfortable speaking fluent English in front of a camera, we decided on recording the movie in German.

Short Movie

Our Idea

The idea behind Between Dice and Madness: A classic Adventure is a typical Dungeons and Dragons round with a lot of chaos and stereotypes. We wanted to portray the group playing the game, but also how the players imagine the scenarios. Therefore, we decided on recording the group playing the game in real life, while we chose a 3D world to display the “theatre of mind”.

We have decided to show the group completing a dungeon, as this idea allowed us to show a whole game session within only 10 minutes and did not require any background information to the world or characters. We were able to jump directly to the important and comical parts of the session and could skip the long fighting scenes and parts, where nothing special happens.

My Part

After our movie shoot and voice-over recordings were over, I started working on the 3D models for the Dungeon. Our Characters were created on the Hero Forge Website, so I only needed to add textures to them. During this time, we also recorded our animated scenes with Motion Capture. For this, I worked mainly as an actor for the Elf Character. The process of modelling and texturing took a while, but after that was done, I helped out on the clean-up of the animations. There we unfortunately encountered a lot of problems, which led to the sometimes inaccurate movements in our final movie. After the animation clean-up, I also helped out with preparing the scenes for the final rendering. I helped to place the characters at the correct positions and did a rough alignment of the animations according to the timing.
